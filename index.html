Robot Overview ‚Äî IMPULSE

Our robot, named IMPULSE, was designed with one core objective: to act quickly, precisely, and reliably in every match scenario. The concept of impulse in physics ‚Äî the transfer of force that sets an object into motion ‚Äî perfectly represents our design philosophy.

Throughout the season, we went through multiple iterations. Early designs suffered from inconsistent launching and mechanical complexity. By simplifying mechanisms, reducing weight, and switching to more stable systems, we achieved higher accuracy and reliability.

IMPULSE features a catapult-based outtake powered by rubber bands, providing consistent launch force compared to unstable flywheel systems. The drivetrain is built on a GoBILDA chassis with mecanum wheels, allowing precise omni-directional movement and smooth control in both autonomous and tele-op.

Failures during early competitions taught us critical lessons in stress distribution, material choice, and system integration. Each redesign made the robot stronger, faster, and more competition-ready.

Competitions & Growth

Bishkek FIRST Regional

At Bishkek, we faced high-level alliances and intense match pressure. Our strengths were drivetrain stability and alliance coordination. Weaknesses included early shooting inconsistency, which we addressed by adjusting launch angles and rubber tension.

Almaty FIRST Regional

This competition highlighted our growth. Improved autonomous routines, better communication, and mechanical reliability led to stronger results. Winning the Control Award validated our engineering decisions.

Daryn Qualifier

Here, we focused on consistency. Although matches were demanding, our robot performed reliably, and we secured Innovate Award 2nd Place, proving our design evolution and documentation strength.
